# Court Handicap — Creative Liberation Proposal

*One vision. The one I believe in. Not a menu.*

---

## The feeling

A court is a trap someone else set. The market draws the lines, picks the favorite,
prices the night, and hands a player an environment he never agreed to. Most nights
the trap holds. Some nights a player **escapes it** — and that escape is the only
thing worth showing.

So Court Handicap should not feel like analytics. It should feel like **evidence of a
fight**. A film-room wall at 2am where someone who can't stop thinking about basketball
has drawn the court, branded the betting line into the floor, crossed out the numbers
the lineup made wrong, carved the pressure with a grease pencil, and slashed the verdict
across the baseline. Precise underneath. Human all over it.

Thesis: **Every court is a trap. Every great game is an escape. Gingeball shows the cut.**

## What the CourtGraph wants to become

Not a court with data on it. A **proof surface**. The market *burns* par into center
court. The lineup *overwrites* it — market par struck through, lineup par scrawled
beside it in ember, because the overwrite **is** the lineup changing the court. The
archetype *carves* pressure: for a scoring guard the paint closes in like wet paint and
arrows bear down on his airspace; flip to the rim protector and the same paint **opens**
— relief, not threat. Same game, redrawn by hand into a different court. The player who
beats it leaves a **scar**: a stroke that cuts up through the par line and breaks the
system open. The one who gets trapped is swallowed by the smear.

## The mark I want to invent

Court Handicap needs its own glyph — the leaderboard has the seam; this needs the
**Breakthrough**: a struck horizontal *par line* with a single stroke cutting up through
it. It's not decoration — it's a variable. Cut **through** the line = beat the court.
Stop **short** of it = trapped. That one gesture is the whole product compressed, and it
recurs everywhere: the verdict seal, the conditions axis, the scar on the CourtGraph.

## Marks that mean things (the rule that keeps this from being cosplay)

Texture is only allowed if it carries meaning. My v2 failed because the grit was
decoration. The discipline now:
- struck-through number = the market was **superseded**
- overwritten par in ember = the **lineup changed the court**
- pressure arrows carved inward = **difficulty bearing down** on this archetype
- wet-paint smear closing in = **suppression / trap**
- a clean sharp rule = **confidence**; a broken/fraying edge = **role conflict / hazard**
- possession tallies struck along the baseline = **proof**, each one a fingerprint
- the scar cutting through the system = the player who **beat the court**

Sharp geometry is the skeleton; the hand is the muscle. *A precise mathematical system
with human hands all over it.*

## The risk I'm taking

I'm drawing the court **by hand** — authored irregular linework with intent, not a
turbulence filter faking grit — and letting the graphic system get a little ugly where
the game is ugly. The market burn is loud. The trap smear is almost too much. The scar
breaks the grid on purpose. If it's wrong, it'll be wrong with a pulse.

## What stays sacred

The math and architecture do not move: the formula registry, the metric/data contracts,
the confidence honesty, the outputs (103·99, suppression 10.9%, **+8.6** beat). Court
Handicap stays its own environment; the court is the graph; market sets par, lineup
shapes it, archetype translates it, possessions prove it, the verdict matters; Gingeball
DNA without cloning TCV.

## What I'd loosen

Two things. One: "never use texture" → **"marks must mean something."** That single edit
frees the whole thing. Two: "objects only, never a literal court" → a **hand-drawn** court
is not the enemy; a *decorated* court was. Let me draw it.

## What makes it fun, and what makes a user feel something

Fun to build: the court literally re-forming under your hand when you switch archetypes,
and inventing a glyph that encodes win/loss in one stroke. What makes a user feel
something: watching the paint close in on a trapped star, then watching another player
cut a scar clean through the same court — and understanding, with no stat training, that
*this game was a different fight for each of them.*

## What I'd make if I were allowed to surprise you

I already started. The CourtGraph swing attached is the proof surface: hand-drawn court,
market burn at center, par overwritten by the lineup, pressure carved per archetype, the
trap smear, the breakthrough scar, possession tallies as evidence, and the new Breakthrough
mark. Switch the player and watch the court get redrawn. Tell me where it makes you feel
something and where it doesn't — then I'll carry this exact language into MarketHub (the
burn), Conditions (the overwrite), and the Verdict (the slash).
